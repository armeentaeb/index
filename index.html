I am a postdoctoral fellow of ETH Foundations of Data Science (ETH-FDS) at ETH Zürich, mentored by Peter Bühlmann. Previously, under the supervision of Venkat Chandrasekaran, I obtained my PhD in the Department of Electrical Engineering at Caltech. During my PhD, I was fortunate to collaborate with Parikshit Shah, Andrew Stuart, and Michael Turmon.

My research interests are at the interface of optimization and statistics and focus on developing practical modeling frameworks that capture complex phenomena, can be optimized efficiently, and are amenable to model selection techniques. More specifically, I work on developing methods for false discovery control in contemporary data analysis settings, and for latent-variable modeling by finding low-rank structures underlying data. Currently, I am particularly interested in exploiting stability and robustness for inferring causal structure.

Together with Matthias Löffler and Yuansi Chen, I co-organize the Young Data Science Researcher Seminar at Zurich .
