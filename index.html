  Armeen Taeb


I am PhD student in the Electrical Engineering option at Caltech, where I am advised by Venkat Chandrasekaran. I have also been fortunate to collaborate with Parikshit Shah, Andrew Stuart, and Michael Turmon at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory. My research focuses on developing and analyzing optimization-based methods to solve problems in statistics. A significant aspect of my work is to develop useful statistical methodlogies for real-world applications. For more details, please visit my CV.

I received my undergraduate degrees in Electrical Engineering and Applied Mathematics from University of Colorado at Boulder. During my undergraduate studies, I worked under Shannon Hughes and Richard Baraniuk.
